SHARED DECISION WORKSPACE

GOAL:
Design a 1-page executive decision workspace for L’Oréal Marketing Orchestration Center.
This is not a passive reporting dashboard.
This is an active decision workspace where signals from multiple platforms come together into one coordinated view so stakeholders can understand what matters now, see trade-offs, and decide what to do next.

PRIMARY USER:
Senior marketing stakeholder / brand lead / media lead / orchestration lead
Example persona: Sophie Marchand, Brand Manager, Lancôme

PLATFORMS TO CONNECT:
1. Integrated Media Intelligence
2. Creator to Commerce ROI Engine
3. Brand-Safe Promotions Lab
4. Trend-to-Plan Demand Sensing
5. Media Influencer Platform

PAGE PURPOSE:
Show how signals from all 5 platforms align into coordinated business action.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Premium futuristic enterprise UI
- L’Oréal black / deep navy background
- Gold for title emphasis and orchestration center highlights
- Use neon accent lines and glows very carefully for key signals
- Clean, sharp, elegant layout
- Executive-ready, high-end, polished
- Strong hierarchy, clean spacing, minimal clutter
- Use Roboto font
- Use Lucide icons
- Global corner radius: 4px
- Avoid generic AI-dashboard look
- Avoid cluttered charts everywhere
- Make it feel like an orchestration cockpit, not a BI report

COLOR MAPPING FOR THE 5 PLATFORMS:
- Integrated Media Intelligence = red/coral
- Creator to Commerce ROI Engine = purple
- Brand-Safe Promotions Lab = orange
- Trend-to-Plan Demand Sensing = cyan/teal
- Media Influencer Platform = blue
- Orchestration / shared workspace center = gold

PAGE LAYOUT:
Create one desktop page with the following structure:

1) TOP HEADER
- Left: L’Oréal Marketing Orchestration Center
- Main page title: Shared Decision Workspace
- Subtitle: Where signals align into coordinated action
- Right side controls:
  - Date range dropdown
  - Brand filter
  - Market filter
  - Campaign filter
  - Export / Share button
  - Profile/avatar
- Include a subtle executive status tag:
  - “3 priority decisions today”
  - “2 conflicts detected”
  - “1 action ready for approval”

2) EXECUTIVE SUMMARY STRIP
A premium summary row with 4–5 compact cards:
- Biggest Opportunity
- Biggest Risk
- Recommended Next Action
- Decision Confidence
- Response Window

Example microcopy:
- Biggest Opportunity: “Creator momentum is rising for Génifique launch”
- Biggest Risk: “Inventory confidence is below safe threshold in FR and UK”
- Recommended Next Action: “Shift paid support to high-converting creator audience clusters”
- Decision Confidence: “86%”
- Response Window: “Next 48 hours”

3) MAIN DECISION ZONE
This is the center of the page.
Design 2 or 3 large “Decision Cards” that combine signals from multiple platforms.

Each decision card should include:
- Decision title
- Short business summary
- Contributing platform signals (as colored chips or indicator dots)
- Business impact estimate
- Risks / guardrails
- AI recommendation
- Quick actions

Example Decision Cards:
A) “Accelerate creator-led support for Génifique”
- IMI: Share-of-voice increasing
- Creator ROI: Top 3 creators outperforming benchmarks
- Media Influencer Platform: Paid amplification ready
- Demand Sensing: Inventory stable in key markets
- Promotions Lab: No promotion required
- Recommended action: Increase support on TikTok and Instagram in top-converting audiences

B) “Delay promotion in DACH to protect margin”
- IMI: Competitor pressure detected
- Promotions Lab: Margin risk flagged
- Demand Sensing: Inventory coverage below safe threshold
- Media Influencer Platform: Paid pressure could increase demand too fast
- Recommended action: Hold discounting, shift to creator-led education

C) “Reallocate media budget to creator-supported conversion path”
- IMI: Paid performance flattening
- Creator ROI: Creator-assisted conversion rising
- Media Influencer Platform: Budget shift available
- Recommended action: Move 12% from generic paid to creator-amplified audiences

4) SIGNAL ALIGNMENT PANEL
Add a clear section that shows how the five platforms contributed to the decision.
This should feel easy to scan.

Show 5 platform columns or a horizontal contribution strip:
- Integrated Media Intelligence
- Creator to Commerce ROI Engine
- Brand-Safe Promotions Lab
- Trend-to-Plan Demand Sensing
- Media Influencer Platform

For each platform, show:
- current signal
- status icon
- confidence
- short note

Use color coding consistently.

5) RISK AND CONFLICT PANEL
Add a section called:
“Conflicts, Risks, and Guardrails”

Show items like:
- Margin risk
- Inventory constraint
- Promotion timing conflict
- Audience overlap
- Channel saturation
- Legal/compliance check

Make this panel feel important but not overwhelming.
Use warning and status styling that is premium and minimal.

6) DECISION ACTION BAR
At the bottom or right side, add action controls:
- Approve
- Hold
- Adjust timing
- Shift budget
- Open platform detail
- Assign to team

This should feel like the workspace is built for action, not just observation.

7) OPTIONAL RIGHT-SIDE AI PANEL
Add a collapsible AI guidance panel titled:
“AI Recommendation Layer”

Include:
- Why this action is recommended
- What changed in the last 24 hours
- What could happen if no action is taken
- Suggested stakeholders to notify

Keep this panel elegant and compact.

KEY UX PRINCIPLES:
- Make the screen feel like one clear decision environment
- Reduce noise
- Prioritize what matters now
- Emphasize alignment across systems
- Show trade-offs, not just metrics
- Make it obvious what decision should happen next
- Use meaningful labels and realistic sample content
- No lorem ipsum

CHARTS / DATA VISUALS:
Use only a few high-value visuals:
- one trend chart
- one platform contribution strip
- one impact estimate mini chart
- one risk indicator module
Do not overload the page with analytics widgets.

MICROCOPY STYLE:
- concise
- executive-friendly
- confident
- professional
- simple English
- business-first, not technical jargon-heavy

INTERACTIONS TO MOCK:
- clicking a Decision Card opens a richer detail panel
- hovering a platform chip shows the source signal
- clicking “Approve” changes the state to approved
- clicking “Open platform detail” routes to that platform’s view
- collapsing/expanding the AI Recommendation Layer
